# Afternoon tea
Emi: "I wanna try making the pound cake."

Ingram: "Okay, that's an easy one to make. It'll be good for familiarizing yourself with basic baking steps."

Ingram: "For this one, the ingredients are pretty simple. There's butter, sugar, eggs and flour."

Ingram: "But we'll spicing it up a little by adding earl grey to it."

Emi: "Ooo, a tea-flavoured cake! That sounds fancy."

*Ingram cleans up the recipe drafts on the table, separating out the pound cake recipe. After storing the drafts in a drawer, he brings out a large mixing bowl.*

Ingram: "Emi, can you measure out these ingredients out? I'm going to make some earl grey milk tea to add to the batter later."

Emi: "Sure, I can do that!"

*I take the recipe from Ingram and begin measuring out the ingredients with a kitchen scale. In the meantime, Ingram boils some milk in a saucepan.*

Emi: "Okay, everything's good to go! What's next?"

Ingram: "Nice timing, I just finished the milk tea. We can make the cake batter while it's cooling down."

*Ingram sets the saucepan on a coaster, leaving it to cool off on its own. The scent of Earl Grey fills the air.*

Emi: "(Mmm, that smells good. I can't wait for how the cake's going to turn out later.)"

*Under Ingram's instruction, I mix all the ingredients together, creating a sticky batter that falls from my whisk in long ribbons.*

Ingram: "Now we just need to add the milk tea and pour this into a baking pan. Then it'll be ready for the oven."

Annelise: "Emi? Ingram? What are you guys doing?"

*Just as I'm about to grab the saucepan, Annelise appears at the kitchen entrance.*

Emi: "Oh, Annelise! Come on in, Ingram's just teaching me to make pound cake for the cafe."

Annelise: "Huh? O-oh, it's okay, I don't want to bother you. . ."

Emi: "No, it's fine! Come see what we're doing!"

*At my insistence, Annelise inches into the kitchen, fidgeting with her fingers. I move over, inviting her to have a look.*

Emi: "Smell that? We decided to add some Earl Grey in. Would you like to be the first one to try it when we're finished?"

Annelise: ". . .!"

*Annelise whips her head towards me, bobbing excitedly. She tugs at my arms, staring directly at me with twinkling eyes.*

Annelise: "May I? I'd love to have a try!"

Emi: "O-okay, I'll make sure you get a slice as soon as it's done then."

*I look at Ingram, confused. He chuckles and shrugs.*

Annelise: "Oh, I just had an idea. . . Can we try putting a bean in?"

Emi: "E-eh? Beans?"

Emi: "Annelise, you like beans in your cake. . .?"

Annelise: "Ah, no, that's not it!"

Annelise: "I was just thinking. . . maybe it'd be fun to play a little game to see who ends up eating the bean."

Emi: "Hmm, that doesn't sound like a bad idea! Can we throw one in, Ingram?"

Ingram: "Sure, I don't see why not."

*As per Annelise's suggestion, we find a kidney bean and throw it into the bowl.*

*Soon enough, the cake goes into the oven and it's time for a quick lunch break.*

Ingram: "Here's some onigiri by the way. I tried to make something quick and easy since we were busy baking."

Emi: "Oh, thanks! I appreciate it."

*Feeling a bit peckish, I happily grab one of the rice balls and dig in. The seaweed crinkles as my teeth sink in, melding with the slightly sticky rice.*

Ingram: "Feel free to have one too, Annelise. There's enough for everyone."

Annelise: "Oh, thank you. . ."

Emi: "So Ingram, why's it called a 'pound cake'? What's so special about it?"

Ingram: "It's because all the ingredients being added weigh one pound each."

Emi: "Huh, that's pretty convenient. I wonder how someone came up with that."

*Annelise straightens her back. For a split second, she opens her mouth, only to sink back into her rice ball.*

Emi: "Hm? Annelise, did you want to say something?"

Annelise: "Eh? N-no, I. . ."

Annelise: ". . . I was just going to say that pound cake was invented a long time ago, when a lot of people couldn't read or write."

Annelise: "Because of how simple the recipe was, they were able to remember the recipe off the top of their head."

Annelise: "That way, they could enjoy cake without needing to read a cookbook."

Emi: "Oh, that's cool! So it's a dessert shared by many people then. Did you know that, Ingram?"

Ingram: "No, this is news to me too. Where'd you learn that, Annelise?"

Annelise: "Oh, I read about it in a history book about tea. It mentioned pound cake as a staple for afternoon tea."

Annelise: "A-actually, speaking of which, would you guys like to try some tea with the cake when it's finished?"

Annelise: "I can make some tea to go with it."

Emi: "That sounds lovely! I'd love to have a sip."

Ingram: "Hmm, now that I think about it, I've never tasted your tea before. I'd like to have a try as well."

Annelise: "Okay!"

*She gobbles up the rest of her rice ball and skips over to the pantry. She comes back and presents two glass jars.*

Annelise: "Here's two of my latest blends. Which of these would you guys like to try?"

Annelise: "One of them is aged tangerine peel, and the other is a blueberry blend."

Ingram: "Hmm, if I recall correctly, Earl Grey pairs well with citrus flavours. The tangerine would compliment the cake."

Emi: "How about the blueberry one? What's its flavour profile?"

Annelise: "It's sweeter, almost like a dessert tea. Similar to Earl Grey, it's also based on bergamot oil."

Annelise: "The blueberry adds a fruity twist, but it might be a bit overbearing with the cake."

Ingram: "I'm fine with either of them. What do you think, Emi?"

Emi: "I want. . ."
